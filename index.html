FisiApp

Previous Next

My group and I went out here to Bogotá looking for problems we could solve. We noticed a lot of people had muscle injuries and they couldn't find a proper way to treat them. Most of them took tons of medical appointments but was really hard to remember all the recommended exercises after the appointment. In order to deal with this, we did an application where anyone could see what kind of exercises could help them depending on their injury. We also added videos showing the steps required to complete each task. They could also track which exercises were completed because the application used the phone's sensors to watch the user's movement. Finally, we added a map with all the medical centers so they could open a WhatsApp's chat and contact them. If they got near a medical center, a notification will pop in their phone.

Semantic Web

Previous Next

The objective of this project was to let anyone search for papers or authors located in a couple of databases around the world. We got the information of thousands of papers and added them to an ontology. The onthology was located in a graphDB server and could be accessed by a website created by us. The site had many functionalities such as filtering by instances, classes, or properties with SPARQL queries. Additionally, it showed graphs of author's connections with D3 and a natural language query.

Mixed Reality

Previous Next

Virtual Reality has been an interesting topic since long ago, so I build an application that could improve students' learning of all ages. Inside the virtual world, they saw a clasroom with a teacher in front of them. While they were there, a short story was told, followed by a couple of questions. Later on, they were instructed to do the same exercise but now with a YouTube video instead of the virtual classroom. The point of the experiment was to prove that students focus more when they were in a virtual environment.
